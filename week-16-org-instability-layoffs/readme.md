# Week 16: Org instability / layoffs (you + your team)

## 🎯 The situation

Something's wrong. Rumors of layoffs, a reorg, a missed quarter, an exec departure. Anxiety is spiking, productivity is cratering, and people are reading tea leaves in every Slack message. If you manage people, your team is watching *you* to calibrate how scared to be. And you have your own fear to manage at the same time.

You need to lead yourself and others through uncertainty — staying composed, honest, and useful when you don't have the answers and can't promise outcomes.

**Constraints:**

- You genuinely don't know what will happen, and can't promise anyone is safe
- False reassurance ("we'll all be fine!") destroys trust the moment it's proven wrong
- Your own anxiety is real and your team can smell performance
- This is managing up (staying informed, calm, useful) *and* down (steadying people) at once

**Suggested time budget:** 15 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write what you'd say to your team** in the first conversation after the news breaks.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the instability walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for what people remember about how you behaved in the crisis.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You're honest about what you know, what you don't, and what you can't promise
- You acknowledge the emotion without amplifying or performing it
- You give people the one thing they can control: the work in front of them
- You protect your own composure — it's the signal the whole team reads
- You manage up too: you stay informed and useful, not just a transmitter of dread
