# Week 06: Giving hard feedback to a report

## 🎯 The situation

Someone on your team - competent, well-liked - keeps doing one thing that's hurting them: they go quiet when they're blocked, then miss dates without warning. It's not a fireable issue. It's a habit that's capping their growth and starting to cost the team. You've hinted twice. The hints didn't land. Now you need to actually say it.

You need to deliver feedback that's clear enough to change behavior but kind enough that they stay motivated and trust you.

**Constraints:**

- This is your direct report; the goal is to grow them, not punish them
- Vague feedback ("communicate more") is useless and they'll nod and forget
- Too harsh and they shut down or start job-hunting
- You've avoided this twice already, which is part of the problem

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes to script the conversation

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write your opening** - the first thing you'd say to start the feedback.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the managing-down feedback walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for what your report reads in *how* you give feedback.

## 💡 What good looks like

- Specific behavior + specific impact, not a character judgment
- One issue, clearly, not a laundry list ambush
- A genuine two-way conversation - you ask, you don't just deliver
- A concrete, agreed change with a check-in, not a vague "do better"
- Warmth and directness together; neither alone is enough
