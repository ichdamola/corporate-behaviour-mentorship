# Week 09: The skip-level meeting

## 🎯 The situation

Your manager's manager wants a 1:1 with you. Maybe it's routine; maybe it's not. Either way it's a rare, high-leverage 30 minutes with someone two rungs up who normally only sees you through a filter. Handle it well and you become a name they remember in rooms you're not in. Handle it badly — by trashing your manager, by going blank, or by over-polishing — and you torch trust in both directions.

You need to make a strong, authentic impression, be useful to them, and not undermine your own manager.

**Constraints:**

- Badmouthing your manager is the single fastest way to lose a skip-level's trust
- "Everything's great!" with no substance wastes their time and reads as scripted
- They want signal: how's the team *really* doing, what do you see from the ground
- Whatever you say may get back to your manager — assume it will

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes to prep

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write your answer** to "So, how are things going?" — the real opening they'll use.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the skip-level walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for what a skip-level is actually probing for.

## 💡 What good looks like

- Honest, specific signal about the work — not a highlight reel, not a complaint reel
- Loyalty *up and down*: you can name a challenge without throwing your manager under
- You come with a point of view on the business, not just status
- You ask one good question that shows you think above your level
- Composed, candid, and forward-looking — they leave thinking "more of that one"
