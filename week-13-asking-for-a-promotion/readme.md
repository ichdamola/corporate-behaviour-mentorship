# Week 13: Asking for a promotion

## 🎯 The situation

You've been operating at the next level for a while — or you think you have. Promo cycle is coming. The mistake most people make is treating the promotion as a *reward they're owed* and the conversation as an *ask*. It isn't. It's a case you build, on the company's terms, with evidence, that you hand your manager so *they* can champion it in a room you're not in.

You need to open the promotion conversation and equip your manager to fight for you.

**Constraints:**

- Your manager argues for you in a committee you'll never see — they need ammunition
- "I've worked really hard" is not a promotion case; *scope and impact* are
- The bar is "already operating at the next level," not "ready to be given a chance"
- Timing, packet, and sponsor all matter as much as the work itself
- **2024–2026 reality:** promotion freezes, no-headcount, downleveling, and "you're operating at the next level but there's no slot this cycle" are common. The packet is necessary but not sufficient — sometimes the answer is "yes, but not this cycle" and the move is repositioning for the next one, not pushing harder this one.

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write how you'd open the conversation** with your manager — the first 3 sentences.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the promotion walkthrough (including the evidence packet).
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for how promo committees actually decide.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You map your work to the *next* level's expectations, with evidence, in writing
- You make the ask early enough to act on gaps before the cycle, not at the deadline
- You give your manager a packet they can paste into a committee, not a vibe
- You ask "what's missing?" and treat the answer as a plan, not a rejection
- Composed and specific — you're presenting a case, not pleading
- You acknowledge the freeze/headcount variable upfront — your packet is for "yes," but your follow-up plan covers "yes, but not this cycle" without losing momentum

## 🧭 If the answer is "yes but not this cycle"

This is now the modal outcome in many orgs, not the exception. Your walkthrough will cover the standard case; this is the branch.

- **Don't treat "not this cycle" as rejection.** It's a deferred yes; protect the asset by getting specifics: which cycle, what evidence will close the gap, who else needs to vouch.
- **Get the timeline in writing.** "We'll revisit in H2" without a date or condition is a slow no. Convert to "I'd like to plan for [specific cycle] with [specific evidence]; can we agree on that as the target?"
- **Build the sponsor network now.** Promo committees give weight to peer-managers and skip-levels who can vouch independently. The next 6 months are about visible cross-team contribution that 2-3 people *outside* your manager will defend in a room.
- **Don't shop yourself externally as the first move** — but do calibrate. If your market value is materially higher than your current band, that's information for the next cycle's case (and worst case, your exit).
