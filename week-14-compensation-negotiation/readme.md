# Week 14: Compensation negotiation

## 🎯 The situation

It's an offer, a raise conversation, or a counter to a competing offer. The number on the table is lower than it should be, and the person across from you is *not* your adversary - they're often constrained by bands and approvals they don't control. Most people lose money here not because they ask wrong, but because they get emotional, anchor low, accept the first number, or make it personal.

You need to negotiate for more while staying calm, factual, and easy to say yes to.

**Constraints:**

- The recruiter/manager has limited room and isn't the enemy
- Anger, ultimatums, and oversharing all weaken your position
- "I need more because rent is high" is not leverage; market data and value are
- The relationship continues after the negotiation - don't scorch it
- **You don't know the band; they do.** That asymmetry shapes every move below. Anchor with market data, but assume they're working from a band you can't see.
- **2024–2026 reality:** in a soft labour market, "competing offer" leverage often doesn't exist and aggressive negotiation gets the offer pulled. Many of the classic moves were written for the 2018–2022 hot market and don't transplant cleanly.

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## 💡 What good looks like

- You anchor with a researched, specific number - and then go quiet
- You negotiate the whole package (base, equity, sign-on, level), not just one lever
- You stay warm and collaborative - "help me get to yes," not "give me or else"
- You let silence do work; you don't talk yourself down from your own ask
- You get the final number in writing before you celebrate or sign
- You calibrate to the market: in a soft market, lean on internal equity, level, and total package; in a hot market, leverage can be more aggressive

## 🧭 If you have no competing offer

The classic negotiation canon assumes you walked in with a competing offer. **Most current negotiations don't have one.** This is the branch.

- **Anchor on the band, not on leverage.** Use Levels.fyi / Pave / public company filings for the actual level + location + company-stage range. The ask becomes "based on the public data for [level] at [company-stage] in [location], the band looks like X–Y; I'd land at the upper end given [scope evidence]."
- **Negotiate the level, not just the number.** A bump from L4 → L5 reshapes the rest of the package and is sometimes available when a base bump isn't.
- **Use "what would yes look like."** Not "I need more" - "what would it take to land at [X]?" puts them on the constructive side of the conversation. It also reveals what they actually have room on (level, sign-on, equity refresh) without you having to guess.
- **Don't manufacture leverage you don't have.** Inventing competing offers gets discovered (recruiters talk; their offer letter has a contingency line) and tanks the relationship. Genuine internal interest from another team can be real leverage; fabricated outside interest rarely is.
