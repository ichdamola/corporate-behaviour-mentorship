# Week 14: Compensation negotiation

## 🎯 The situation

It's an offer, a raise conversation, or a counter to a competing offer. The number on the table is lower than it should be, and the person across from you is *not* your adversary — they're often constrained by bands and approvals they don't control. Most people lose money here not because they ask wrong, but because they get emotional, anchor low, accept the first number, or make it personal.

You need to negotiate for more while staying calm, factual, and easy to say yes to.

**Constraints:**

- The recruiter/manager has limited room and isn't the enemy
- Anger, ultimatums, and oversharing all weaken your position
- "I need more because rent is high" is not leverage; market data and value are
- The relationship continues after the negotiation — don't scorch it

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write your counter** — the exact words you'd use to ask for more.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the negotiation walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for the tactics that move numbers and the ones that backfire.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You anchor with a researched, specific number — and then go quiet
- You negotiate the whole package (base, equity, sign-on, level), not just one lever
- You stay warm and collaborative — "help me get to yes," not "give me or else"
- You let silence do work; you don't talk yourself down from your own ask
- You get the final number in writing before you celebrate or sign
