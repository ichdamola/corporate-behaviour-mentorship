# Week 12: Managing up to a chaotic / absent manager

## 🎯 The situation

Your manager is disorganized, slammed, or checked out. Priorities change weekly, decisions stall waiting on them, your 1:1s get cancelled, and you can't get a clear answer when you need one. None of this is malicious - they're underwater. But their chaos is becoming *your* missed deadlines and *your* stalled growth.

You need to manage upward: create structure that makes their job easier and your work unblockable, without it reading as a power grab or a complaint.

**Constraints:**

- They're overloaded, not malicious - solutions, not grievances
- You can't fix their calendar, but you can change how you interface with it
- Going over their head torches the relationship unless you've truly exhausted options
- Decisions die in their inbox - you need a way to force lightweight closure

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write the message** you'd send to create structure (e.g. a weekly priorities recap, a decision-forcing format).
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the manage-up-to-chaos walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for how to look reliable *because* of the chaos, not despite it.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You make decisions easy: default recommendations they can approve in one word
- You create a lightweight written cadence (weekly recap, "I'll proceed unless you object by X")
- You absorb the right amount and route the rest - you don't become a martyr or a bottleneck
- You document agreed priorities so the weekly whiplash has a paper trail
- You stay neutral and helpful; the chaos never shows up in *your* tone
