# Week 07: The underperformer (the PIP conversation)

## 🎯 The situation

A report has been underperforming for two quarters. You've given feedback, set expectations, offered support. It hasn't moved. You're now at the point where you need a formal performance conversation — possibly a Performance Improvement Plan. This is the hardest managing-down situation there is: high stakes for them, legal and HR implications, and a real human on the other side who may be scared, defensive, or blindsided despite your earlier signals.

You need to have a conversation that is honest, documented, fair, and humane — that gives a real chance to recover while protecting the team and yourself.

**Constraints:**

- This must be documented and consistent with prior feedback (no surprises)
- HR is involved or should be; you are not freelancing this
- The person may react with anger, tears, or bargaining — you stay neutral
- "Nice but vague" here is actually cruel: it denies them a real chance to fix it

**Suggested time budget:** 15 minutes to plan

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write how you'd open the conversation** and the one-sentence summary of where they stand.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the performance-conversation walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for the legal/HR realities and what *not* to say.

## 💡 What good looks like

- A clear, unambiguous statement of the gap — no euphemism, no hedging
- Grounded in documented prior feedback; nothing is a surprise
- Specific, measurable expectations and a real timeline
- Composed and humane throughout, even if they aren't
- Everything in writing; HR aligned before you walk in
