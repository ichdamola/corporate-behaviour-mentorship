# Week 07: The underperformer (the PIP conversation) - Walkthrough

> ⏱️ **Time budget:** see the situation in [readme.md](readme.md)
> 🎯 **Goal:** Move through the 10 moves with composure. You don't need a perfect script - you need the *rhythm*.
>
> 📝 **Status: scaffold.** Weeks 01–03 are written out in full as the template for voice and depth. This week gives you the 10-move skeleton and the specific things to nail. Fill it in as you work the situation - or open an issue / ask for the full walkthrough.

## 1. Read the room
*Who's actually the audience here? What's the power map - up, down, or sideways? What do they actually want vs. what they're saying?*

> 💬 **How to say it:** *(internally)* "..."

## 2. Name your goal
*What outcome do you want from THIS interaction - stated as a result, not a feeling. What does "handled well" look like in one sentence?*

## 3. Separate fact from feeling
*Strip the emotion. What are the plain, neutral facts? What's the feeling that generated them - and why it shouldn't be the thing you lead with.*

## 4. Pick the channel
*Sync or async? 1:1 or group? Verbal or written? Match the channel to the weight and sensitivity of the moment.*

## 5. Headline first
*Lead with the bottom line. (With a manager on a charged topic, "headline" may mean asking before asserting - see Week 02.)*

> 💬 **How to say it:** "..."

## 6. Stay neutral
*The move that carries everything. No edge, no apology spiral, no performance. Clean and factual so it can't be twisted.*

> 💬 **How to say it:** "..."

## 7. Leave a receipt
*Put the decision / agreement / next step in writing - as a calm recap, never a gotcha.*

> ✉️ **The written version:** "..."

## 8. Route it up or down
*Does this belong with your manager (up) or your report (down)? Surface it / delegate it instead of silently absorbing it.*

## 9. Hold the boundary
*Confirm the ask, the no, or the next step - once, calmly, and hold it under push.*

## 10. Close the loop
*Follow through. Make the resolution visible. Connect this moment to the next so you read as reliable.*
