# Week 04: Delivering bad news / a missed deadline

## 🎯 The situation

The feature your manager promised to *their* boss won't be ready Friday. It's now Tuesday. You've known the slip was likely since last week but kept hoping you'd recover. You won't. Your manager is about to look bad upward, and how you deliver this determines whether you look like a problem or a professional.

You need to surface the slip *now*, with a plan — not an apology spiral, and not a surprise on Friday.

**Constraints:**

- The later they find out, the worse it lands — surprise is the real failure
- Your manager has to reforecast upward, so they need specifics, not vibes
- "It's not my fault" is true but irrelevant and reads as junior
- There's a recoverable version (a smaller scope ships Friday)

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes to draft the heads-up

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write the message or opening line** that delivers the slip — verbatim.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the bad-news walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for how senior people deliver slips vs. how junior people do.

## 💡 What good looks like

- Headline first: the slip, the new date, in the first sentence
- Brings a plan and options, not just a problem
- Owns the timeline without groveling or blame-shifting
- Gives your manager what they need to manage *their* upward conversation
- Lands early enough to be recoverable, in writing so it's on record
