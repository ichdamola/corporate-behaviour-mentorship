# Week 11: The difficult peer

## 🎯 The situation

A peer - same level, different strengths - is consistently friction. Maybe they're dismissive in reviews, maybe they relitigate settled decisions, maybe they're passive-aggressive in writing where everyone can see. You have no authority over them. You'll work together for years. Escalating to your managers makes you both look like you can't operate. Letting it ride means it gets worse and starts costing the work.

You need to defuse the dynamic and establish a workable equilibrium - sideways, without a boss to adjudicate.

**Constraints:**

- No authority either way; this is pure lateral influence
- Escalating prematurely makes *you* look like you can't manage a peer
- Their behavior may be a symptom (pressure, insecurity) you can't see
- The written record is public - your replies are part of your reputation too

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write the message or opening line** for a direct, low-heat conversation with the peer.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the difficult-peer walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for when to absorb, when to address directly, and when to escalate.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You go direct and private first - never litigate the peer in a public thread
- You name the *pattern and its impact on the work*, not their personality
- You stay relentlessly neutral in writing, so the record always favors you
- You look for the shared goal and offer a working agreement, not a verdict
- You know your escalation threshold *before* you need it
