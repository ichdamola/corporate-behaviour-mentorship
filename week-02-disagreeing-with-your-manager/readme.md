# Week 02: Disagreeing with your manager

## 🎯 The situation

Your manager has decided the team will rebuild a service from scratch instead of refactoring the existing one. You think it's the wrong call — the rewrite will take two quarters, the existing system is fine with cleanup, and you've seen this exact decision sink teams before. The decision isn't final yet, but your manager is clearly leaning hard one way and seems invested.

You need to register your disagreement in a way that's heard, not dismissed as "negative" — and you need to be able to live with the outcome either way.

**Constraints:**

- You report directly to this person; the relationship matters long-term
- You have genuine expertise here, but so does your manager
- Other team members privately agree with you but won't say so
- The decision affects the next 6 months of your team's work

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes to plan the conversation

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write out how you'd open the conversation** — the first 3 sentences, verbatim.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** to compare against the canonical "disagree and commit" walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for what your manager is reading about *you* in how you disagree.

## 💡 What good looks like

- Frames it as a shared goal, not a turf fight ("we both want X")
- Leads with the strongest *fact*, not the strongest *feeling*
- Asks before asserting ("what's driving the rebuild?" before "the rebuild is wrong")
- States the disagreement once, clearly, then commits — no relitigating in Slack
- Leaves a written record of the concern, framed as input, not "I told you so" insurance
