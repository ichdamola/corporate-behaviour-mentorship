# Week 10: Pushing back on an unrealistic deadline

## 🎯 The situation

Leadership has committed to a date. The date is not real. You can see the math: the scope, the team size, and the calendar don't reconcile, and everyone in the room is nodding anyway. If you stay quiet, you own a death march and a miss with your name on it. If you push back wrong, you're "not a team player" and "negative."

You need to make the constraint visible and force a real tradeoff conversation - scope, time, or people - without becoming the person who said it couldn't be done.

**Constraints:**

- "It can't be done" is a wall; leadership hears it as you, not the math
- The date may be externally committed (a customer, a board) - context you may lack
- The tradeoff is real: something has to give, and that's a *decision*, not a complaint
- You need this in writing so the eventual reforecast isn't pinned on you

**Suggested time budget:** 10 minutes

## ✅ Your job

1. **Write how you'd raise it** - the framing sentence that opens the tradeoff, verbatim.
2. **Then open [answer.md](answer.md)** for the deadline-pushback walkthrough.
3. **Read [cues.md](cues.md)** for how to push back so it reads as ownership, not resistance.

## 💡 What good looks like

- You bring the *math*, not the verdict - let the numbers make the case
- You frame it as a menu of tradeoffs (cut scope / move date / add people), their choice
- You assume there may be context you lack and ask before declaring
- You commit hard to whatever they choose - disagree, then row
- A written recap of the agreed tradeoff so the reforecast is shared, not yours alone
